Reproduction of Issue #2119 / #2095 — Stacking Context Overlap

The virtualizer's transform: translateY() creates a CSS stacking context. Inside it, each turn (<section>) paints in DOM order. Turn N's sticky header is z-index: 20 within its own section, but Turn N+1's entire section paints on top of Turn N because it appears later in the DOM.

Result: When scrolling to the bottom, Turn N+1's sticky header overlaps the action buttons and bottom content of Turn N's assistant message, making messages appear cropped/misaligned.

Scroll the chat panel below to see the overlap. The later turns' content paints over earlier turns.

How to verify the issue:
1. Scroll down so the bottom-most messages are visible
2. Notice that later turns' content paints on top of earlier turns' content
3. The action buttons at the bottom of each assistant message may be partially or fully hidden under the next turn's sticky header
4. Toggle "Debug Mode" to see the turn boundaries more clearly
5. Click "Highlight Overlap Zones" to see which areas overlap

Root cause: The virtualizer's transform: translateY() (line 1201 of MessageList.tsx) creates a CSS stacking context. Each turn's section { position: relative } creates a child stacking context. Later turns paint on top of earlier turns because they appear later in the DOM. The z-index: 20 on sticky headers only applies within each turn's stacking context, not across turns.
